A gesture of gratitude

A philosophical conversation over a fancy dinner, a comforting pep-talk during a walk in the park, or a long overdue reunion with high school friends: life is full of memorable moments with people that matter to you. Such interactions can easily be taken for granted, while it may be difficult or daunting to show your gratitude with words or gifts.

The Thank You Token is a concept I came up with to thank the important people in my life. I don't like gift-giving, as gifts often are unwanted and because of its environmental impact, and at the same time I find it difficult to convert my grateful feelings into words. The idea of a simple token of appreciation, holding little intrinsic value but all the more sentimental value, helps me in thanking close friends and family.

What makes a token?

A Thank You Token could be anything, but it typically has little intrinsic value and is preferably decorated (for example by painting) with our logo to make it stand out. Ideally, you shouldn't need to buy something to make a token. Simple wooden blocks, smooth rocks, and even bottle caps work, as do DIY crafts such as origami and paper mache. Ultimately, the purpose of the token is the sentimental value it carries - the look should not matter too much.

How to give one

There's no need to wrap the token in gift wrapping: just give it along with some kind words and our Thank You Token flyer, which helps explain the concept to those unfamiliar with it and contains a link to the web app.

How does the web app work?

In the app, you can write down the story behind each received/given token along with a picture, which is stored securely in your own Google Drive.
